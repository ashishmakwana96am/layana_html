Seven steps of Technology

Step 1 : Hydro Peel/Deep Cleanse

Uses a combination of three solutions to cleanse and smooth the skin using AHA & BHA based solutions. It hydrates and nourishes the skin by infusing lactic, salicylic and hyaluronic acid to deeply cleanse, purify and hydrate. 

Step 2 : Exfoliation

Provides deep exfoliation by buffing the skin using an exfoliation capsule containing active ingredients, which results in clearer, hydrated and brighter skin. This step triggers the Bohr effect encouraging the blood to deliver oxygen to the skin.

Step 3 : Oxygenation

This step encourages maximum absorption allowing intense skin hydration. Applying oxygen to the skin with this method will help increase cell metabolism. Some bacteria are not able to live in oxygen so applying the oxygen to the skin will give this anti-bacterial effect

Step 4 : Skin Lifting

This treatment uses electroporation which increases the absorbency of the cell for product penetration. This, in turn, revitalises and 'wakes up' dull and lifeless skin.

Step 5 : Skin Tightening

Radio frequency technology is used to increase collagen reproduction in mature skin. Skin tightening is ideal for incorporating into an anti-ageing facial.

Step 6 : Hydration

The ultrasound technology used in this step aids the product infusion and increases hydration in the deeper layer of the skin.

Step 7 : Fire and Ice

This step incorporates heat to open pores, increase blood supply, aid product infusion and strengthen the skin. The cold applicator decreases the blood supply, tightens skin, and seals the infused products and also helps to reduce redness following the treatments.

Preparation and Aftercare

Preparation
Aftercare

  • Avoid excess sun exposure, tanning beds for1 week before treatment

  • Do not use any type of exfoliants 2 days before treatment.

  • Refrain from any chemical peels or lasers for 2 weeks prior.

  • Discontinue use of Tretinoin type products (Renova, Tretinoin, Retin A, Retin A Micro, Tri-Luma, Solage, etc.) at least 2 days before treatment.

  • If you have a history of cold sores, consider beginning prophylactic treatment with Valtrex or similar no later than the day of treatment.

  • Notify us if you develop a cold sore, acne, open lesions in the area being treated, or experience any type of illness before your treatment.

  • Wait 2 weeks after Botox and filler treatments to have a HydraFacial treatment.

  • Avoid active skin preparations for 3-5 days before Hydro2facial

_Avoid active skin preparations for 3-5 days pre and post Hydro2

  • Avoid sun exposure 3 days post treatment, hat, sun umbrella + SPF is a must

  • Drink plenty of water

  • Makeup may be applied the day after treatment.

  • Avoid excessive heat 3 days post treatment, ie heavy workouts, steam rooms, saunas and sun beds

  • Avoid chlorine for 24 hours

  • Avoid any exfoliation to the area for 48 hours post-treatment.

Your therapist will sit with you during a consultation to discuss your facial concerns and will then be able to advise which facial and technologies are best suited to help you.
