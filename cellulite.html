What is Cellulite?

Cellulite is a condition in which the skin has a dimpled, lumpy appearance. It usually affects the buttocks and thighs but can also occur in other areas.This benign condition mostly occurs in women due to differences in fat distribution and is a very common problem with 80-90% of women having some degree of cellulite, also known as orange-peel skin, due to its texture.

Despite how common a problem cellulite is, the exact cause of cellulite is not known. Many factors are thought to contribute to it however no single lifestyle change – diet or exercise is able to eradicate it. How is cellulite classified?

There are 3 grades of cellulite. To determine what stage you are, look into a mirror over your shoulder and examine your buttocks and thighs.

Grade 1 (mild): Cellulite (dimpling) is visible when the skin is pinched (or alternatively when the underlying muscles are contracted).

Grade 2 (moderate): Cellulite (dimpling) is visible while standing.

Grade 3 (severe): Cellulite (dimpling) is visible while lying down (supine).
