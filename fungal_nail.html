How does the treatment work?

Discolouration and sun damage are a key sign of ageing, with an increase in pigmented lesions you will experience uneven skin tone and a lack of radiance. Preparation and Aftercare

Preparation
Aftercare

  • Prior to treatment, we suggest you visit a Chiropodist/Podiatrist to confirm the diagnosis of fungal nail, and have the nail paired down if necessary. Please bring a spare pair of clean socks to wear after your treatment.

  • Wash feet every day and dry well between the toes.

  • Apply Dactarin, or another anti-fungal cream, twice a day, morning and night after washing feet.

  • Keep nails short and cut straight across and always cut infected nails last.

  • Disinfect nail clippers and scissors after use and soak in bleach.

  • Always wear clean socks daily and wear clean socks at night time as the fungus can spread onto bed clothes.

  • Where possible, wear open toe sandals without socks whilst treatment continues however,if you must wear enclosed shoes, always wear clean socks.

  • Wear flip flops or equivalent when showering or in public bathing areas.

  • Do not share towels with anyone.

  • Disinfect shower / bathroom floor with bleach.

  • Wash bedding, socks, tights, towelsÂ etc. on a 60 degree wash.

  • If possible throw old shoes and slippers away and disinfect shoes with anti-fungal product frequently.

  • Limit nail polish use â€“ this can trap moisture and worsen the infection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Light is absorbed by the water in the skin underneath and around the nail, creating heat. This heat helps to destroy the dermatophytes and eliminate the fungal infection. The laser applicator is placed on the nail and a short pulse of light is released. The entire nail bed and nail matrix is then treated in this way.

A warm sensation and some discomfort are felt during treatment. However, a skin cooling device can be used to make treatment as comfortable as possible.

The number of treatments required varies, although typically 2-6 treatments are needed for optimum results.

Results become apparent as the nail grows out. Due to the slow rate of toenail growth it may take several months for the results to be seen.

Most people are suitable for treatment. We recommend a consultation with your clinician to ascertain if the treatment will be successful for you.
